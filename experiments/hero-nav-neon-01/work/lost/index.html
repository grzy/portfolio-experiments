A field guide for birds at the edge of silence  +  a Figma prototype that almost went extinct itself.

99.9% of all species that have ever lived are extinct.

The conversation around it is so heavy that people check out before they connect.

Lost & Endangered doesn’t ask you to grieve.
It asks you to listen.

◆ the problem

Doom framing hits a wall.

Audience testing confirmed it: lead with tragedy and people disengage before they connect. Sadness + helplessness + fatigue + the tab closes.

Extinction needed a quieter narrative. A voice going silent.

Not doom. Disappearance. A voice gone quiet.

Sound is the strongest trigger of memory. If extinction is a voice going silent, remembering becomes the act of listening.

◆ what was built

Four capabilities, one listening room.

  1. 01

    The archive directory

    Enter through the lost birds. The directory is the home screen -- every species on the edge of silence catalogued in one place, sorted by what remains: original recording, AI-reconstructed song, or nothing at all.

  2. 02

    Bird profiles with a bioacoustic archive

    Every species gets a profile. Original recording when one exists -- AI-reconstructed song when the bird went quiet before we started listening. Flora AI handles the imagery, giving extinct birds a visible presence again.

  3. 03

    Immersive ecosystems

    Choose a habitat. The UI dissolves. You sit inside the sound -- fiordland rain, rainforest canopy, the northwest coast at dusk -- the world as it was before we started counting what was missing.

  4. 04

    Soundscape composer

    Drag and drop. Layer bird calls with weather, time of day, the silence between. Save your composition as a conservation stamp.

❖ kaua’i ‘ō’ō

The bird this case story was built around.

Moho braccatus  +  last heard 1987

A Wednesday morning, May 1971. Field biologist John Sincock heard a call he’d only ever read about  +  within fifteen feet of him, a pair of ‘ō’ō thought extinct for years.

He returned with his wife Renate and a heavier kit. She took the first photos of the species ever captured. Through the summers that followed, the team made extensive recordings  +  hoped.

“Knowing the pair might well be the only ones left on the planet, he recorded every detail of their feeding, flight, and call.”

— Daniel Lewis, Counting Extinction, The Huntington

Last seen 1985. Last heard 1987. Hurricane Iniki devastated Kaua’i in 1992. After many thousands of years, the Kaua’i ’ō’ō was gone.

L&E immersive experience  +  Kaua’i 'ō'ō song  +  Alaka’i ambience.

❋ pivot · spotted owl

When the audio becomes a weapon.

Second species anchor. Weeks looking for usable audio.

What I found was hard to sit with  +  poachers use the owl’s call to track them down. Recordings get weaponized.

The page stayed in the prototype. The audio stayed out.

Some recordings shouldn’t be in a soundscape.

✢ reflection

A project that almost went extinct.

Lost & Endangered finished in Figma. Weeks later, Figma Make shipped  +  vibe coding went baseline. Static prototypes became a kind of fossil overnight.

This case story is what happens when I refuse to let a project go quiet.

The research held. The thesis held. The Kaua’i ’ō’ō is still worth listening to. What changed was the medium.

What I’d build next

  • Rive for the ecosystem dissolves  +  real fades, not state-swaps.
  • A coded listening room where the UI actually recedes.
  • Conservation stamps that mint as shareable SVGs.
  • Mobile companion for field listening  +  record, identify, layer.

The version in Figma is the one that almost went extinct.
The version in code is the one that keeps breathing.
